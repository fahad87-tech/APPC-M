Part (a) — Graph Sketch

Sketch a graph of the total kinetic energy vs distance traveled as the cylinder rolls down the incline (0 → L₀) and across the horizontal surface (L₀ → 2L₀).
Height drops linearly with d: h = H₀ − d·sin θ. By energy conservation KE = m₀g·d·sin θ — linear in d. On flat, no work → KE = constant at m₀gH₀. Graph: line from (0,0) to (L₀, m₀gH₀), then horizontal to 2L₀.

Part (b) — Energy Explanation

A block of mass m₀ slides down a separate rough incline (same L₀, H₀). Both reach the bottom with the same translational speed. Explain why in terms of energy.
Cylinder: ¾m₀v² = m₀gH₀ → v² = 4gH₀/3. Block: ½m₀v² = m₀gH₀ − |W_f|. Equal v requires |W_f| = ⅓m₀gH₀ = cylinder's KE_rot.
