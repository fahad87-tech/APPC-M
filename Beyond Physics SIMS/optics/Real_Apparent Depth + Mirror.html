🔬 Virtual Image Convergence

Tank Depth (H): 4.00 m
Fish Depth: 2.00 m
Mirror Image (I₁): 6.00 m
Apparent Image (I₂): 4.50 m
Ray Visibility
Real: Fish→Mirror→Surface→Eye
Virtual: I₂→Surface→Eye (straight)
All virtual rays converge at I₂
Parameters
Answer: d) 9H/8
Step 1: Mirror image at depth = 2H - H/2 = 3H/2

Step 2: Apparent depth = (3H/2) × (3/4) = 9H/8
dapparent = (2H - d₀) × (n₂/n₁)
= (3H/2) × (3/4) = 9H/8
Key Concept

Virtual rays (magenta) appear to come from I₂ and travel in straight lines to the eye. All virtual rays converge at I₂ (the apparent image), showing where the eye perceives the fish to be. Real rays (yellow/orange/cyan) bend at the surface.

(a) The depth of the virtual image formed by the mirror. [2 pts]
(b) The apparent depth of this image as seen by an observer above the water. [3 pts]
(c) The ratio of apparent depth to actual tank depth. [2 pts]

Question 2: Conceptual Analysis (5 points)

Explain why the apparent depth of the fish's image is greater than the actual depth of the fish (d₀), despite the fact that refraction normally makes objects appear shallower than they are. Your explanation should address:

(a) The role of the mirror in creating the optical path. [2 pts]
(b) Why the mirror image is located below the actual fish. [3 pts]

Question 3: Experimental Design (7 points)

Question 4: Multiple Representations (5 points)
